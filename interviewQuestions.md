# Questions Form or For Interviews

## Winco dev
- Why do you want to work here?

- Tell us about yourself?
    - I graduated in 2025 July
    - I have been looking for a software job and am currently working at winco to pay the bills
    - I have wanted to work in software since I was in high school

- What is an achievement your proud of from work?

- How would you proceed if you didn't know how to solve a problem?
    - I would spend 20 - 30 minutes researching documentation and the code base, if I still can't figure out the issues I would look up some targeted questions for 10 - 20 minutes. If still stuck, I would reach out to a team member or manager for help.

- What would you do if you have finished your tasks but can't get a hold of your manager?
    - The Answer: "I’d start by checking the backlog for 'low-hanging fruit' or technical debt that needs addressing. I might also improve documentation or write unit tests for the code I just finished. I’ll send a quick Slack/email to my manager saying: 'I'm done with X, so I'm starting on Y until I hear from you.'"

- How would you deal with a problum beteen you and your team member?
    - The Answer: "I believe in addressing things early and privately. I’d ask them for a quick chat to understand their perspective. Usually, conflicts are just miscommunications about technical implementation. I try to focus on what’s best for the project, not who is 'right'."

- How would you deal with a co-worker leaking or discussing  confidential data?
    - The Answer: "Security and data integrity are top priorities. I would first remind the coworker of our NDAs/policies if it seemed accidental. However, if it’s a serious breach, I am obligated to report it to my manager or the security/HR department immediately to protect the company and our users."

- How would you go about diagnosing a bug?
    1. Reproduce: Find the exact steps to make the bug happen.
    2. Isolate: Check the logs, network tab, or debugger to see where the data breaks.
    3. Simplify: Strip away code until only the failing part remains.
    4. Fix & Test: Apply the fix and ensure no regressions were created.

- What is a project your proud of or struggled with?
    - I am proud of the fact that I am trusted to get the job done. There are several times when I have been put into a role I am not rrained for but have been trusted to get done when short handed. My  manager knows that I can and will get things done and do them as efficiently as I can with the skills and knowledge I currently posses.
