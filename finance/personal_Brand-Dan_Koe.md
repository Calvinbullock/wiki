## Monitization
- Performance – they understand how to articulate ideas in an engaging way. The ideas have the potential to "do well."
    - do other like it?
- Excitement – they have a genuine interest in understanding the idea or articulating it in their own way. They see their brand as a collection of notes of these ideas.
    - Do you want or write about it?
    - is it interesting to you?
    - are you at the edge of your understanding and growing?

## Trust Matrix
- 1. Growth -- doing what works
- 2. Authenticity -- expressing core beliefes
- 3. Authority -- displaying expertise

### Authenticity
- People don't follow ideas they follow people who share ideas
- two people can share the shame idea and people will perceive it in drastically different ways
- _Time under attention_ – The more attention people give you, especially in long form content like articles, books, and videos, the more they perceive your posts through the rest of your ideas.
    - this is why most new people quite they don't give themselves enough time to amass a reach
- _Alignment of values_ – When people know who you are, where you came from, and what you stand for, they form a deeper relationship with your ideas as a whole.
- _Authentic polarization_ – "If you're liked by everybody, you're liked by nobody." You need to give people reasons to heavily disagree and thus heavily agree with you. This is the difficult part of putting yourself out there.

- We need to illustrate our story and core beliefs often in our content, both as content topics and as ways of reframing ideas.
- Write out where you were, what sparked change, and where you are now.
- For each topic or theme you talk about, list out core principles that you hold with conviction.
